Cricket tournament

The Cricket World Cup is organized by the International Cricket Council (ICC), the global governing body for cricket. The tournament features national teams from around the world competing for the title of world champions. The format of the World Cup has evolved over the years, but it typically involves a series of group stage matches followed by knockout rounds, culminating in a final to determine the winner.

Some key points about the Cricket World Cup include:

1) Frequency : The ICC Cricket World Cup is held approximately every four years.

2) History: The first Cricket World Cup was held in 2007 in South Africa. West Indies emerged as the inaugural champions.

3) Participation: The tournament usually involves teams from Test-playing nations, as well as associate and affiliate members of the ICC.

4) Trophy: The winners of the Cricket World Cup are awarded the ICC Cricket World Cup Trophy. The trophy has undergone changes over the years.
